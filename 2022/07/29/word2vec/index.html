What is Wordnet

Wordnet is a thesaurus which contains a list of synonym sets and hypernyms.

​ synonyms: words that have nearly the same meaning 同义词，近义词

​ hypernyms: is-a relationships 上位词

​ nltk package 内嵌了Wordnet

Advantages and problems with resources like Wordnet

  1. 基于规则构建的词表，准确性高？？Not always good because it lacks contexts!
  2. Hard to keep it up-to-date
  3. Build by human labor which could be subjective
  4. Can’t compute accurate word similarity

How to represent words to enable machine readability?

Tradictional way: represent words as discrete symbols

One-hot vectors representation

  1. The vector dimension is the size of the vocabulary
  2. Limitations: could not compute similarity; vectors are very sparse(contain a lot of zeros)

Modern statistical NLP: distributed representation

  1. Representing words by their contexts
  2. Embedding the similarity within vectors
  3. Create a dense vector for each word
  4. Word vectors also known as word embeddings or neural word representations

Word2Vec

basic concepts and key ideas

Word2vec, proposed by Mikolov in 2013, is a framework for learning word vectors(word embeddings).

There are some important concepts helping you to understand the key idea of Word2Vec:

  1. center word at position t
  2. context words in window size of j
  3. window size j

CBOW Continuous Bags Of Words: Given a bags of context words, calculate the probability of a center word

Skip-grams model: Given a center word c, how to calculate the probability of a context word o that accompanies by the center word: $P(o|c)$ ?

Answer: compute the similarity between a center word c and a context word o. (Important note: there are two word vectors per word $w$: one as a center word and one as a context word. why two vectors: easier optimization, average both at the end)
$$
P(o|c) = \frac{exp(u^T_ov_c)}{\sum_{w\in V}{exp(u_w^Tv_c)}}
$$
$v_c$: word vector for a center word c

$u_o$: word vector for a context word o

$exp$: Exponentiation makes anything positive

$\sum$: nomalize over the entire vocabulary to give probability distribution

$V$: vocabulary size in a corpus

w: each word in the fixed vocabulary

$u_w^T$: the transpose of the word vector for w

softmax function have the same format:
$$
softmax(x_i) = \frac{exp(x_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^nexp(x_j)} = p_i
$$
The softmax function maps arbitary values $x_i$ to a probability distribution $p_i$: $R^n\to(0,1)^n $

The objective function, also known as cost function or lost function is
$$
J(\theta)= -\frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^T\sum_{-m\leq j\leq m \atop j\neq 0}\log(P(w_{t+j }|w_t;\theta))
$$
How to minimize the objective function $J(\theta)$ (maximize the probability)?

  • Gradient descent
  • Stochastic gradient descent (SGD): in order to overcome the limitation of gradient descent
